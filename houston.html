Houston's History

Houston was founded in 1837 by brothers, Augustus Chapman Allen and John Kirby Allen. Months after the Texas War the brothers named the city after General Sam Houston. Sam Houston was a general in the army and president in the replublic of Texas and helped win Texas independence from Mexico.

Houston is now one of the most ethinically and culturely diverse cities in the United States. It is also known as the energy capital as it is the center for the oil and gas industry. Houston is also home of NASA Johnson Space Center, where the control center is located.
