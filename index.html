About

Texas is a state in the southern part of the United States. It is bordered by the Gulf of Mexico. It is the second largest state in the United States and the 33rd largest in the world. Texas is known for its bustling cities, its diverse culture, and its rich history.

Texas became the 28th state in 1845.

Geography

Texas can be divided into four main geographical regions.


The Coastal Plains, the largest region, extend from the Gulf Coast into the state's interior, featuring rolling hills and coastal prairies. Bordered by the Rio Grande River to the west and the Balcones Escarpment to the northwest, it's prone to hurricanes. The Great Plains, drier and treeless, sit at a higher elevation and are known as the High Plains. To the east and north lie the North Central Plains, less arid but mostly prairies with unique landforms like mesas. Finally, the Mountains and Basins region in the west boasts the most mountainous terrain, including parts of several mountain ranges, and features desert-like conditions with cacti.
